## Foundations & References

- [Economics Stack Exchange](https://economics.stackexchange.com/) – Community Q&A on economic theory and methods.
- [CORE-Econ](https://www.core-econ.org/) – Open-access economics textbooks.
- [NBER](https://www.nber.org/) – Leading source of economic research papers.
- [Journal of Economic Perspectives](https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep) – Accessible analysis of economic research.

## Macroeconomics

- [FRED](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/) – Extensive macroeconomic time-series database.
- [IMF Data](https://www.imf.org/en/Data) – Global macroeconomic and financial statistics.
- [World Bank Data](https://data.worldbank.org/) – Development and macroeconomic indicators.
- [OECD Data](https://data.oecd.org/) – Economic statistics for developed economies.
- [Penn World Table](https://www.rug.nl/ggdc/productivity/pwt/) – Cross-country productivity and income data.

## Microeconomics

- [Experimental Economics Database](https://exptdata.org/) – Data from controlled economic experiments.
- [Consumer Expenditure Survey](https://www.bls.gov/cex/) – Household spending data from the U.S. BLS.
- [IO Economics Resources](https://people.stanford.edu/shapiro/) – Industrial organization datasets and papers.

## Econometrics & Modeling

- [Statsmodels](https://www.statsmodels.org/) – Econometric modeling in Python.
- [R econometrics packages](https://cran.r-project.org/web/views/Econometrics.html) – Comprehensive list of R econometrics tools.
- [Stata](https://www.stata.com/) – Statistical software widely used in economics.
- [EViews](https://www.eviews.com/) – Econometrics and forecasting software.
- [Gretl](https://gretl.sourceforge.net/) – Open-source econometrics software.

## Economic Data Sources

- [IPUMS](https://ipums.org/) – Harmonized census and survey microdata.
- [Bureau of Labor Statistics](https://www.bls.gov/) – Employment, inflation, and wage data.
- [Eurostat](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat) – European Union economic statistics.
- [UN Data](https://data.un.org/) – Global economic and social indicators.
- [Open Economic Data](https://openeconomicdata.org/) – Index of open economic datasets.

## Computational Economics

- [Dynare](https://www.dynare.org/) – DSGE modeling platform.
- [QuantEcon](https://quantecon.org/) – Open-source computational economics tools.
- [JuliaEconomics](https://github.com/JuliaEconomics) – Julia packages for economic modeling.
- [PyEcon](https://github.com/jessegrabowski/py-econ) – Python tools for economic simulation.

## Policy & Development Economics

- [J-PAL](https://www.povertyactionlab.org/) – Evidence-based policy research.
- [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/) – Data-driven insights on global development.
- [AidData](https://www.aiddata.org/) – Development finance and aid datasets.
- [World Inequality Database](https://wid.world/) – Income and wealth inequality data.

## Financial & Monetary Economics

- [BIS Statistics](https://www.bis.org/statistics/) – Global banking and monetary data.
- [Federal Reserve Publications](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres.htm) – Monetary policy research.
- [CRSP](https://www.crsp.org/) – Financial market datasets.
- [Fama-French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/data_library.html) – Asset pricing factors.

## Visualization & Analysis Tools

- [pandas](https://pandas.pydata.org/) – Data analysis library used in empirical economics.
- [ggplot2](https://ggplot2.tidyverse.org/) – Grammar-of-graphics plotting in R.
- [Plotly](https://plotly.com/) – Interactive economic data visualizations.
- [Tableau](https://www.tableau.com/) – BI tool used for economic dashboards.

## Learning Resources

- [MIT OpenCourseWare – Economics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/economics/) – University-level economics courses.
- [Marginal Revolution University](https://mru.org/) – Short, high-quality economics lessons.
- [Econometrics Academy](https://sites.google.com/site/econometricsacademy/) – Advanced econometrics lectures.
